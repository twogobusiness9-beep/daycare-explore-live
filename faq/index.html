Frequently Asked Questions

Everything Bozeman families ask about Montessori, our programs, and enrolling at Explore.

About Explore

Explore Montessori & Academy is an infant, toddler, preschool, and early education program in Bozeman, Montana. We are more than childcare — we provide nurturing care and authentic Montessori early education for children from infancy through age 6.
We have two Bozeman campuses: Explore Montessori at 1933 N 22nd Ave, Bozeman, MT 59718, and Explore Academy at 1705 W Kagy Blvd, Bozeman, MT 59715.
Explore serves children from infancy through age 6. Programs include Explore Piccolo for infants, Pre-Primary for toddlers, and Primary for preschool and kindergarten-aged children.

Montessori Philosophy

Explore combines the time-tested Montessori philosophy with the practical support modern families need — reliable hours, flexible schedules, classroom cameras, and daily communication.
Freedom within limits means children are given real choices within clear, respectful boundaries. This builds independence, self-discipline, and confidence.
A prepared environment is a calm, intentional classroom designed around the child — low shelves, child-sized furniture, and hands-on materials that invite independent, purposeful work.
Yes. Mixed-age classrooms are part of the Montessori approach. They allow younger children to learn from older peers, and older children to reinforce learning by mentoring.

Programs

Explore Piccolo is our infant program. It supports the physical, emotional, and social development of our youngest learners. We are licensed to serve infants beginning at 6 weeks old.
Pre-Primary serves children from around 14 months to 3 years old in a thoughtfully prepared environment focused on practical life, movement, and language.
Primary serves children ages 3 to 6, including preschool and kindergarten-aged learners, across language, sensorial, math, cultural studies, and practical life.
Yes. Our Primary program serves children ages 3 to 6 and includes kindergarten-aged learners in a mixed-age Montessori classroom.

Whole-Child Development

Yes. Nature is one of our core values. We emphasize unplugged experiences through gardening, outdoor play, and hands-on exploration of the natural world.
Yes. At Explore, play is understood as powerful and developmentally important, especially in the Pre-Primary years.
We approach behavior as part of child development. Teachers use compassion, positive reinforcement, and consistent routines to help children build self-regulation.

Parent Partnership

We use Brightwheel for daily notes, naps, meals, photos, activities, two-way messaging, and incident reports. Classroom cameras are also available.
